AUTHENTIC CHICKEN CURRY

Ingredients

  1. Bone-in Chicken
    • An authentic Indian chicken curry is cooked with a whole chicken cut into pieces. Bone-in meat is the essence of Indian meat curries. I highly recommend using whole chicken cut into pieces for this recipe. Boneless chicken breast is a good substitute for bone-in chicken pieces.
  2. Tomatoes
    1. Roma
    2. Vine
      • I usually like to use pureed Roma or Vine tomatoes. Remove the seeds before pureeing them so you don't have to sieve them
  3. Ginger & Garlic
    • Grate the ginger and garlic or mince them in a food processor. I like to make and store large batches of ginger-garlic paste. It comes in handy in my kitchen.
  4. Onion
    • I like to thinly slice the yellow onion for chicken curry. That's how my mother did. You may run it through the chopper if you want.
  5. Oil
    • A generous amount of oil is a sign of rich and authentic Indian curry. I am using ½ cup of oil but you may adjust according to your desired quantity.
  6. WHOLE & GROUND SPICE
    1. Whole cinnamon sticks
    2. Bay leaves
    3. green cardamom
    4. clova
    5. Black peppercorns
    6. salt
      • I am using pink salt and measurements are based on pink salt. Use salt per your taste and the type of salt you may use.
    7. Gram masala
      • I only use homemade garam masala for meat curry recipes. My homemade garam masala is the key ingredient in this recipe. Whenever a South Asian has tried my chicken curry, they have always asked for the recipe for my garam masala. It's a game-changer, I promise!

Steps to make it

  • Marinade the boneless chicken breast pieces with all the listed powdered spices, ginger-garlic, and whole spices
  • Heat a heavy-bottom pan over high heat. Add the oil to the pan. Once the oil starts to heat up, add chopped onions, and ½ teaspoon of salt to the pan.
  • Turn the heat to medium and sauté the onions until golden. It takes about 5 to 8 minutes.
  • Add marinated chicken to the pan. Sauté until chicken is no longer pink.
  • Add tomato puree and stir well.
  • Cover and cook for 15 to 20 minutes or just until the chicken is tender.
  • Garnish with chopped cilantro and serve it with plain rice, roti, or naan.
